Metatarsal shaft fracture

Foot

ED role
Separate the benign from the dangerous. Tuberosity avulsion and an isolated central shaft fracture with acceptable alignment go home. A Jones fracture, a Lisfranc injury, a displaced first metatarsal, multiple displaced metatarsals, an open fracture, and crush with compartment syndrome do not. Evaluate for a Lisfranc injury on any midfoot or metatarsal-base trauma.
Hallmark
Location on the proximal fifth metatarsal dictates prognosis, and a metatarsal-base injury must not distract from a Lisfranc injury.

DefinitionFracture of a metatarsal in the foot. The fifth metatarsal is the most commonly fractured. Central metatarsals 2 to 4 fracture at the shaft or neck, the first metatarsal is a special high-load bone, and the proximal fifth metatarsal is divided into prognostic zones.

MechanismDirect crush from a heavy object or motor vehicle, twisting with the forefoot fixed, inversion with plantarflexion that avulses the fifth metatarsal base, repetitive stress in the second or third metatarsal, or axial load on a plantarflexed foot that injures the Lisfranc joint.

Diagnosis

  • Localized pain, swelling, and tenderness over the involved metatarsal.
  • Pain on weight-bearing, often inability to bear weight.
  • Plantar ecchymosis of the midfoot suggests a Lisfranc injury.
  • Tenderness at the fifth metatarsal base after an inversion injury.
  • Palpate the entire metatarsal and the tarsometatarsal joints.
  • AP, lateral, and oblique foot radiographs, weight-bearing views when a Lisfranc injury is suspected.
  • Low threshold for CT for stress fracture, Lisfranc injury, or surgical planning.

Imaging

ViewsAP, lateral, oblique, weight-bearing, CT

  • keyTransverse fracture line perpendicular to the metatarsal shaft
  • Fleck sign: avulsion fragment in the first intermetatarsal space indicating Lisfranc injury
  • Loss of alignment between the medial border of the second metatarsal and middle cuneiform
  • Tarsometatarsal diastasis or dorsal displacement of the metatarsal bases
  • Proximal fifth metatarsal fracture line running transverse to the shaft (vs. parallel apophysis)

Classification

TypeCharacteristicsTreatment
Zone 1, tuberosity avulsion (pseudo-Jones)Avulsion of the styloid by the peroneus brevis and the lateral band of the plantar fascia, proximal to the fourth-fifth intermetatarsal joint. Good blood supply, heals well.Hard-soled shoe or walking boot, weight-bearing as tolerated, symptomatic care.
Zone 2, Jones fractureMetaphyseal-diaphyseal junction extending into the fourth-fifth intermetatarsal facet. Vascular watershed with high nonunion risk.Non-weight-bearing short-leg cast 6 to 8 weeks, or intramedullary screw in athletes, displaced fractures, or nonunion.
Zone 3, proximal diaphyseal stress fractureProximal diaphysis distal to zone 2, repetitive load, intramedullary sclerosis. Poor healing.Non-weight-bearing immobilization, or screw fixation for delayed union or nonunion.
Central metatarsal 2 to 4 shaft or neckShaft or neck fracture, the second metatarsal is the most rigidly fixed.Stiff-soled shoe or boot if alignment acceptable, reduce or refer if over 10 degrees dorsoplantar angulation, over 3 to 4 mm translation, or any rotation.
First metatarsalBears the highest load, no ligament tethers it to the second metatarsal base.Low threshold for ORIF, non-weight-bearing, near zero tolerance for malalignment.

Treatment

Non-operative

  • Zone 1 avulsion, hard-soled shoe or walking boot, weight-bearing as tolerated, 3 to 6 weeks.
  • Jones zone 2, non-weight-bearing short-leg cast 6 to 8 weeks if treated nonoperatively.
  • Zone 3 stress fracture, non-weight-bearing immobilization, union may take up to 20 weeks.
  • Central 2 to 4 shaft with acceptable alignment, stiff-soled shoe or boot, weight-bearing as tolerated.
  • Ibuprofen 400 mg by mouth every 6 hours and acetaminophen 1000 mg by mouth every 6 hours for analgesia.

Operative

  • Intramedullary screw for Jones fractures in athletes, displaced fractures, or nonunion.
  • ORIF for displaced first metatarsal, displaced intra-articular fractures, or multiple displaced shafts.
  • Lisfranc injuries are usually operative, ORIF or primary arthrodesis.

Operative when: Lisfranc injury, displaced first metatarsal, multiple displaced metatarsals, displaced intra-articular fractures, Jones fractures in athletes, open fractures, and nonunion.

PediatricThe normal fifth metatarsal base apophysis runs parallel to the shaft, do not call it a fracture. Iselin disease is a traction apophysitis of the fifth metatarsal base from peroneus brevis pull, treated conservatively.

Disposition

Consider discharge when
  • isolated zone 1 (tuberosity) avulsion
  • isolated central shaft fracture with acceptable alignment
  • immobilization in walking boot or stiff-soled shoe
  • weight-bearing as tolerated
Refer / transfer now if
  • Lisfranc injury
  • open fracture
  • displaced first metatarsal
  • multiple displaced metatarsals
  • crush with suspected compartment syndrome
  • Jones fracture (including athletes for screw discussion)
Follow-up
  • fracture clinic in 1 to 2 weeks
  • re-image at approximately 1 week
  • re-image at 4 to 6 weeks
  • graded weight-bearing
  • counsel Jones patients on nonunion risk
  • counsel Jones patients on operative option

Key numbers

The fifth metatarsal is the most commonly fractured metatarsal, roughly half to two thirds of metatarsal fractures in adults.Jones fracture nonunion with nonoperative care is about 15 to 30 percent, up to about 33 percent in active patients.Operative fixation of a Jones fracture gives about 95 to 100 percent union.A non-weight-bearing radiograph series is normal in up to 50 percent of Lisfranc injuries.On weight-bearing or comparison views, Lisfranc diastasis over 2 mm, or over 1 mm versus the other side, indicates injury.

Pitfalls

  • The normal fifth metatarsal apophysis, parallel to the shaft in ages 9 to 14, is mistaken for a fracture.
  • Missing a Lisfranc injury by relying on non-weight-bearing films, which are normal in up to half of cases.
  • Calling a Jones fracture a benign avulsion and undertreating a watershed injury.
  • Anchoring on an ankle injury and missing a fifth metatarsal base fracture.
